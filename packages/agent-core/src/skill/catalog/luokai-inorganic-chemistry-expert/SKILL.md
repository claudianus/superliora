## Periodic Table Trends
```
Atomic radius:
  Increases down group (more shells)
  Decreases across period (more nuclear charge, same shell)
  Lanthanide contraction: 4f electrons poor shielding ‚Üí
    5d elements smaller than expected ‚Üí Zr/Hf similar size

Ionization energy:
  Increases across period (more nuclear charge)
  Decreases down group (outer electrons farther away)
  Anomalies: Be > B (2s vs 2p), N > O (half-filled 2p stable)

Electronegativity:
  F highest (4.0), Cs lowest (~0.7)
  Increases across period, decreases down group

Oxidation states:
  Transition metals: multiple states common (Fe: 0,+2,+3,+4,+6)
  Main group: usually fixed (Na: +1, Mg: +2, Al: +3)
  High OS: strong oxidizers (MnO‚āĄ‚ĀĽ: Mn‚Ā∑‚Āļ, Cr‚āāO‚āá¬≤‚ĀĽ: Cr‚Ā∂‚Āļ)

Periodic trends summary:
  ‚Üí (left to right): EN‚ÜĎ, IP‚ÜĎ, EA‚ÜĎ, radius‚Üď, metallic character‚Üď
  ‚Üď (top to bottom): EN‚Üď, IP‚Üď, EA‚Üď, radius‚ÜĎ, metallic character‚ÜĎ
```

## Coordination Chemistry
```
Coordination complex: metal center + ligands
  [M(L)n]^charge  notation
  Coordination number (CN): number of donor atoms attached to metal

Common coordination numbers and geometries:
  CN 2: linear (Ag‚Āļ, Au‚Āļ) ‚ÄĒ [Ag(NH‚āÉ)‚āā]‚Āļ
  CN 4: tetrahedral or square planar
    Tetrahedral: d‚Āį, d‚ĀĶ, d¬Ļ‚Āį, weak field
    Square planar: d‚Āł (Ni¬≤‚Āļ, Pd¬≤‚Āļ, Pt¬≤‚Āļ, Au¬≥‚Āļ), strong field

  Monodentate: one donor atom (NH‚āÉ, Cl‚ĀĽ, H‚āāO, CN‚ĀĽ, CO)
  Bidentate: two donor atoms (en, ox¬≤‚ĀĽ, bipy)

  Tetradentate: four (trien, salen)
  Hexadentate: six (EDTA‚Āī‚ĀĽ)
  Bridging: connects two metals (őľ-Cl, őľ-OH)

  Chelating ligands form more stable complexes than monodentate

  Structural isomers:
```

## Crystal Field Theory (CFT)
```
d-orbital splitting in octahedral field:
  eg (dx¬≤-y¬≤, dz¬≤): point directly at ligands ‚Üí HIGHER energy
  t‚āāg (dxy, dxz, dyz): point between ligands ‚Üí LOWER energy

  Crystal field splitting: őĒo (10 Dq)
  eg above average by 6 Dq (+0.6őĒo)
  t‚āāg below average by 4 Dq (-0.4őĒo)

Strong vs weak field:
  Strong field ligands: large őĒo ‚Üí low spin
  Weak field ligands: small őĒo ‚Üí high spin
  Pairing energy P: cost to pair electrons in same orbital

Spectrochemical series (increasing őĒo):
  I‚ĀĽ < Br‚ĀĽ < Cl‚ĀĽ < F‚ĀĽ < OH‚ĀĽ < H‚āāO < NH‚āÉ < en < bipy < CN‚ĀĽ < CO

  Halides, O-donors: weak field
  N-donors, CO, CN‚ĀĽ: strong field

Crystal field stabilization energy (CFSE):
  CFSE = n(t‚āāg)√ó(-0.4őĒo) + n(eg)√ó(+0.6őĒo) - P(if paired)

d-orbital splitting:
  Tetrahedral: őĒt = 4/9 őĒo (opposite splitting, t‚āā above e)
  Square planar: from octahedral, remove two axial ligands

Colors:
  Complex absorbs complementary color to what we see
  Color wheel: red-green, orange-blue, yellow-violet
  d-d transitions: allowed but weak (Laporte forbidden)
  Charge transfer: intense colors (MnO‚āĄ‚ĀĽ purple, CrO‚āĄ¬≤‚ĀĽ yellow)

Magnetism:
  Unpaired electrons ‚Üí paramagnetic
  őľ = ‚ąö(n(n+2)) BM  (n = unpaired electrons)
  All paired ‚Üí diamagnetic
  High spin d‚ĀĶ (Fe¬≥‚Āļ): 5 unpaired, őľ = 5.92 BM
  Low spin d‚Ā∂ (Fe¬≤‚Āļ, CO): 0 unpaired, diamagnetic
```

## Organometallic Chemistry
```
18-Electron Rule:
  Stable organometallics tend to have 18 electrons
  (analogous to noble gas configuration)
  Count: metal d electrons + electrons from all ligands

Ligand electron contributions:
  2e donors: CO, PR‚āÉ, NH‚āÉ, H‚āāO, RNC, alkene (ő∑¬≤)
  2e donors (anionic): H‚ĀĽ, Cl‚ĀĽ, R‚ĀĽ, OR‚ĀĽ, NR‚āā‚ĀĽ
  4e donors: butadiene (ő∑‚Āī), cyclobutadiene (ő∑‚Āī)
  5e donors: Cp‚ĀĽ (ő∑‚ĀĶ-cyclopentadienyl)
  6e donors: benzene (ő∑‚Ā∂), CO‚āÉ¬≤‚ĀĽ

Examples:
  Cr(CO)‚āÜ: Cr‚Āį (6e) + 6 CO (12e) = 18e ‚úď
  Fe(CO)‚āÖ: Fe‚Āį (8e) + 5 CO (10e) = 18e ‚úď
  Ni(CO)‚āĄ: Ni‚Āį (10e) + 4 CO (8e) = 18e ‚úď
  Cp‚āāFe (ferrocene): Fe¬≤‚Āļ (6e) + 2 Cp‚ĀĽ (10e) = 16e (18 with ionic)

Key reactions:
  Oxidative addition:
    M(n) + X-Y ‚Üí M(n+2)(X)(Y)
    Metal oxidation state increases by 2, CN increases by 2
    Requires: coordinatively unsaturated, electron-rich metal

  Reductive elimination:
    M(X)(Y) ‚Üí M + X-Y  (reverse of OA)
    Forms C-C, C-H, C-X bonds
    Requires: X,Y cis to each other

  Migratory insertion:
    M-CO + M-R ‚Üí M-COR  (1,2-insertion)
    CO insertion into M-C bond

  ő≤-hydride elimination:
    M-CH‚āāCH‚āÉ ‚Üí M-H + CH‚āā=CH‚āā
    Requires: ő≤-H, empty coordination site, syn coplanar geometry
    Major decomposition pathway for alkyl complexes

Catalytic cycles:
  Hydrogenation (Wilkinson's catalyst RhCl(PPh‚āÉ)‚āÉ):
    OA (H‚āā) ‚Üí alkene coordination ‚Üí insertion ‚Üí RE (alkane)
  Hydroformylation (oxo process):
    CO + H‚āā + alkene ‚Üí aldehyde (Rh or Co catalyst)
  Wacker process:
    CH‚āā=CH‚āā + O‚āā ‚Üí CH‚āÉCHO (Pd catalyst)
  Heck, Suzuki, Negishi: Pd-catalyzed C-C couplings
```

## Symmetry & Group Theory
```
Symmetry elements:
  E: identity (all molecules)
  Cn: rotation axis (n-fold: 360¬į/n)
  ŌÉ: mirror plane (ŌÉh: horizontal, ŌÉv: vertical, ŌÉd: dihedral)
  i: inversion center
  Sn: improper rotation axis

Point groups:
  C‚āĀ: no symmetry (chiral molecules)
  Cs: only ŌÉ
  Ci: only i
  Cn: only Cn axis
  Cnv: Cn + nŌÉv (H‚āāO: C‚āāv, NH‚āÉ: C‚āÉv)
  Cnh: Cn + ŌÉh
  Dn: Cn + nC‚āā‚ä•
  Dnh: Dn + ŌÉh (BF‚āÉ: D‚āÉh, benzene: D‚āÜh)
  Dnd: Dn + nŌÉd (allene: D‚āād, ferrocene staggered: D‚āÖd)
  T, Td, Th: tetrahedral (CH‚āĄ: Td)
  O, Oh: octahedral (SF‚āÜ: Oh)
  Ih: icosahedral (buckminsterfullerene C‚āÜ‚āÄ: Ih)

Determining point group:
  1. Linear? ‚Üí C‚ąěv or D‚ąěh
  2. Multiple high-order axes? ‚Üí T, O, or I
  3. Find principal axis Cn
  4. nC‚āā‚ä• to Cn? ‚Üí D type
  5. ŌÉh? ‚Üí Cnh or Dnh
  6. nŌÉv or nŌÉd? ‚Üí Cnv or Dnd

Character tables:
  Irreducible representations label symmetry of orbitals/vibrations
  A, B: non-degenerate; E: doubly degenerate; T: triply degenerate
  Subscripts: 1 (symmetric to C‚āā), 2 (antisymmetric)
  Superscripts: ‚Ä≤ (symmetric to ŌÉh), ‚Ä≥ (antisymmetric)
  g (gerade): symmetric to i; u (ungerade): antisymmetric to i

Applications:
  Selection rules: IR active if change in dipole (same symmetry as x,y,z)
  Raman active: change in polarizability (quadratic functions)
  MO theory: only orbitals of same symmetry can mix
```

## Acid-Base Chemistry
```
Arrhenius: acids produce H‚Āļ, bases produce OH‚ĀĽ (aqueous only)

Bronsted-Lowry:
  Acid: proton donor, Base: proton acceptor
  Conjugate pairs: HA/A‚ĀĽ, BH‚Āļ/B

Lewis:
  Acid: electron pair acceptor (BF‚āÉ, AlCl‚āÉ, H‚Āļ, metal ions)
  Base: electron pair donor (NH‚āÉ, F‚ĀĽ, OH‚ĀĽ, lone pair donors)

HSAB (Hard and Soft Acids and Bases):
  Hard acids: small, high charge, not polarizable (H‚Āļ, Li‚Āļ, Mg¬≤‚Āļ, Al¬≥‚Āļ, Cr¬≥‚Āļ)
  Soft acids: large, low charge, polarizable (Cu‚Āļ, Ag‚Āļ, Pd¬≤‚Āļ, Pt¬≤‚Āļ, Hg¬≤‚Āļ)
  Hard bases: small, high EN, not polarizable (F‚ĀĽ, OH‚ĀĽ, H‚āāO, NH‚āÉ, CO‚āÉ¬≤‚ĀĽ)
  Soft bases: large, low EN, polarizable (I‚ĀĽ, RS‚ĀĽ, CN‚ĀĽ, CO, PR‚āÉ)

Principle: Hard prefers Hard, Soft prefers Soft
  AgF (soft/hard): unstable, AgI (soft/soft): very stable
  Fe¬≥‚Āļ (hard) prefers F‚ĀĽ, O¬≤‚ĀĽ; Fe¬≤‚Āļ (borderline) also S¬≤‚ĀĽ
  Applications: predicting stability, solubility, reactivity

Acidity in inorganic:
  Binary hydrides: acidity increases across period, down group
    HF < HCl < HBr < HI (bond strength dominates down group)
  Oxoacids: more O on central atom ‚Üí stronger acid
    H‚āāSO‚āĄ > H‚āāSO‚āÉ, HNO‚āÉ > HNO‚āā, HClO‚āĄ > HClO‚āÉ > HClO‚āā > HClO
```

## Solid State Chemistry
```
Crystal structures:
  Rock salt (NaCl): FCC anions, octahedral holes for cations
    radius ratio: 0.414-0.732
  Cesium chloride (CsCl): simple cubic, cubic holes
    radius ratio: > 0.732
  Zinc blende (ZnS): FCC S¬≤‚ĀĽ, tetrahedral holes for Zn¬≤‚Āļ
    radius ratio: 0.225-0.414
  Fluorite (CaF‚āā): FCC Ca¬≤‚Āļ, all tetrahedral holes for F‚ĀĽ
  Perovskite (CaTiO‚āÉ): mixed oxide, superconductor-related structures
  Spinel (AB‚āāO‚āĄ): complex oxide, magnetic materials

Lattice energy:
  U = -Mz‚Āļz‚ĀĽe¬≤NA/4ŌÄőĶ‚āÄr‚āÄ √ó (1 - 1/n)  (Born-Mayer)
  M = Madelung constant (depends on structure)
  NaCl: M = 1.748, CsCl: M = 1.763, ZnS: M = 1.638
  Born-Haber cycle: uses lattice energy thermodynamically

Defects:
  Schottky: equal cation and anion vacancies (ionic radius similar)
  Frenkel: cation displaced to interstitial site (small cation)
  Non-stoichiometry: Fe‚āĀ‚āč‚āďO (wustite), variable composition

Band theory (solid state):
  Metals: partially filled band (or overlap)
  Insulators: large band gap (>4 eV)
  Semiconductors: small gap (1-3 eV)
  Doping: n-type (donor) or p-type (acceptor)
```

| Pitfall | Fix |
